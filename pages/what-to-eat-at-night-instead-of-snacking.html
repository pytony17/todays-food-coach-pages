Simple Strategy

  • 1. Stock your fridge with healthy options like Greek yogurt, fresh fruit, or mixed nuts. Having satisfying and nutritious snacks on hand can help curb cravings without the guilt.
  • 2. Create a 10-minute evening ritual that doesn’t involve food. Consider light stretching, reading, or preparing for the next day to divert attention away from snacks.
  • 3. Set a cut-off time for eating after dinner. Establish a routine so your body gets used to not eating late, giving you clarity and control.

What This Looks Like

Picture this: It’s a Wednesday, and after a long day of meetings, you finally get home. You’re exhausted, and dinner was takeout again. The kids have left snacks on the counter, and your stress levels bubble up. You feel pulled towards those chips or cookies because they’re quick and easy. Instead, you could have opted for a few slices of apple with almond butter, but after a day like today, a healthy choice feels like another chore. You want convenience, but that doesn’t have to mean unhealthy. Finding a balance is possible.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Relying on processed snacks without checking what they actually contain — they can lead to overeating.
  • 2. Ignoring your body’s water signals and mistaking thirst for hunger, which often happens when you’re tired.
  • 3. Waiting too long between meals, leaving yourself vulnerable to giving in to unhealthy late-night cravings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don’t have time to prepare healthy snacks?

Preparation can be as simple as sticking to three go-to options, like yogurt, nuts, or veggies, which you can always have ready to grab.

Aren’t all snacks bad at night?

Not necessarily! Healthy snacks can actually help manage cravings and set you up for success the next day.

I’m not a cook. Can I still manage my eating habits?

Absolutely! There are plenty of easy-to-prepare options that require minimal cooking skills — you just need to know where to look.
