Simple Strategy

  • 1. Keep ready-to-eat snacks, like nuts or yogurt, handy for when you're in a rush.
  • 2. Focus on batch cooking one day a week. Prepare basic proteins and veggies that you can mix and match during the week.
  • 3. Choose simple meals you can assemble in minutes—like salads or wraps—with pre-prepped ingredients.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don't have time to prep meals?

You can still eat well by choosing convenient yet nutritious foods. Consider options like prepackaged salads or frozen veggies, which can be a quick and healthy base for meals.

How can I stay on track when I eat out?

Look for healthier choices on the menu, such as grilled items, salads with protein, or vegetable sides. You can often find something nutritious even when dining out.

What can I do if I'm too tired to cook?

Have simple, nutritious snacks available for when you're too tired to make a full meal. Foods like Greek yogurt, whole-grain wraps, or protein bars can help keep you on track without the hassle.
