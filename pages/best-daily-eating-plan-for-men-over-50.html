Simple Strategy

  • 1. Focus on whole foods: Fill your plate with veggies, lean proteins, and whole grains—keep it simple and colorful.
  • 2. Meal prep for the week: Spend a couple of hours on the weekend preparing easy, healthy meals so you’ll always be ready to eat well.
  • 3. Stay hydrated: Make water your go-to drink, and keep a refillable bottle on hand to remind you to drink throughout the day.

You skip it and grab a soda from the vending machine, which leaves you starving. After a long day, you’re too tired to cook, so you order takeout. You know you want to do better, but between meeting deadlines and dashing to after-work commitments, nutritious choices can fall by the wayside. It’s easy to see how days like this happen, but there’s a better path forward.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Relying on takeout too often: It's quick, but usually packed with calories and sodium. These meals can sabotage your weight loss goals.
  • 2. Skipping meals: When you're busy, it's easy to forget to eat. But waiting too long can lead to poor food choices later.
  • 3. Drinking calories: Sugary drinks or excessive alcohol might not seem like a big deal, but they can add up and derail weight loss efforts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I make time for meal prep?

Block out just two hours on the weekend, and prepare simple meals for the week. It doesn’t have to be complicated or time-consuming.

What if I don’t like cooking?

No worries! Meal prepping can involve easy tasks, like chopping vegetables or assembling salads and wraps. You can also opt for quick recipes that require minimal cooking.

Can I still eat out?

Absolutely! Just aim for healthier options when dining out and practice portion control. A balanced meal can still be enjoyed even on a busy schedule.
