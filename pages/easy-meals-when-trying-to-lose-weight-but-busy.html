Simple Strategy

  • 1. Keep it simple. Aim for easy recipes with 5 ingredients or fewer. Think stir-fries, salads, or oven-baked proteins with veggies.
  • 2. Plan ahead. Dedicate just 15 minutes a week to batch-cook a couple of meals so you’ve got healthy options ready during busy days.
  • 3. Make use of convenience. Stock up on frozen veggies, pre-marinated proteins, and microwaveable grains. They save time without sacrificing nutrition.

What This Looks Like

Imagine this: It’s a Thursday after work, and you just want to collapse on the couch. You glance at the dinner options and feel defeated. You could cook, but you don’t have the energy. So, you end up ordering a large pizza because it’s quick and easy. The thought of prepping a salad feels impossible, and even leftovers seem daunting. You tell yourself you’ll do better tomorrow, but the cycle repeats. Sound familiar? It’s hard to break this routine when life keeps throwing curveballs.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Relying too heavily on takeout because it's convenient, leading to unhealthy choices.
  • 2. Skipping meals during the day, which leaves you starving at night and reaching for whatever is around.
  • 3. Overcomplicating meal prep with complex recipes that take too much time and energy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really have to cook every day?

Not at all! Focus on batch cooking one or two meals each week, and you can eat healthier with less daily effort.

What if I don't like cooking?

You can still eat healthy by using convenience items like pre-cooked proteins and frozen veggies. It’s all about making smart choices.

Will meal planning take a lot of time?

Not at all! Just 15 minutes a week can set you up for success and save you time during busy days.
