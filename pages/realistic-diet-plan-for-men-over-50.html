Simple Strategy

  • 1. Prioritize protein at every meal to feel fuller and reduce cravings. Think eggs, chicken, or legumes.
  • 2. Choose one or two meals a week where you cook in bulk. This makes it easier to grab healthy options when you're busy.
  • 3. Keep healthy snacks handy—nuts, yogurt, or cut fruits—so you’re not tempted by unhealthy choices when hunger strikes.

What This Looks Like

Imagine this: It's a weekday, and you’re running late for work. You grab a quick breakfast—a protein bar—but forget to pack lunch. At 1 PM, your coworker brings in pizza, and you can't resist. After work, you’re too exhausted to cook, so you order takeout. It's not a perfect day, but that's real life. You might not have time for elaborate meals, but with some tweaks to pick healthier options, you can still make progress without the stress.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Skipping meals when you’re busy, leading to overeating later.
  • 2. Relying too much on processed snacks because they’re convenient.
  • 3. Ignoring hunger cues because you're focused on other responsibilities.
