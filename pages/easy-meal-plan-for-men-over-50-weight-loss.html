Simple Strategy

  • 1. Prioritize protein: Start each meal with a protein source—like grilled chicken, fish, or beans—to keep you feeling full.
  • 2. Use one-pan recipes: Look for simple, nutritious recipes that can be made in one pan for easy cleanup and quick cooking.
  • 3. Plan for leftovers: Cook extra at dinner so you have easy lunches ready for the next day—saving time and avoiding unhealthy choices.

What This Looks Like

Imagine it’s a typical Tuesday: you rush to work without breakfast because you hit snooze too many times. Lunch comes around, and you’re too busy to leave the office. You grab a burger and fries from the nearby diner. On the way home, you get caught in traffic—by the time you get back, you’ve lost your appetite and opt for a few snacks instead. Not ideal, right? But this is what many men your age experience daily. It’s about finding a way to make healthy choices without adding more stress to your already packed schedule.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Relying on convenience foods: They’re quick but often packed with hidden sugars and unhealthy fats.
  • 2. Skipping meals: Going too long without eating can lead to overeating later and worse food choices.
  • 3. Not having a go-to meal: Without simple meal options in mind, you're more likely to settle for whatever’s easy.
