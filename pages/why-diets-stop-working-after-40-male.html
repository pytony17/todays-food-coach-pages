Simple Strategy

  • 1. Prioritize protein: Make sure every meal includes a good source of protein. It helps keep you full longer and can be as easy as grabbing a Greek yogurt or a handful of nuts.
  • 2. Plan for leftovers: Double up recipes when you cook, so you have healthy meals ready for those nights when you’re too tired to cook.
  • 3. Stay hydrated: Drink water before meals. It’s an easy way to curb hunger without having to count calories or track anything.

What This Looks Like

Imagine this: it’s Tuesday, and you’ve had a long day at work. You’re tired after back-to-back meetings and just want to crash on the couch. For dinner, you grab takeout on the way home because cooking feels like too much effort. You order what you always do—maybe pizza or burgers—because it’s easy. It hits the spot, but you end up feeling bloated and regretful afterward. This kind of day happens, and it’s normal. But with a few simple shifts, your approach doesn’t have to derail your goals.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Skipping meals: In a rush, you might skip breakfast or lunch, thinking you'll save calories, but that often leads to overeating later.
  • 2. Relying on convenience foods: Quick meals can be tempting, but they often pack in hidden sugars and fats that can sabotage your efforts.
  • 3. Ignoring portion sizes: Without realizing it, you may be serving larger portions, which contributes to excess calorie intake without even noticing.

These strategies work best when personalized to your lifestyle and goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I struggle to lose weight after 40?

As life gets busier and your body changes, old habits can make weight loss feel more challenging. Stress, fatigue, and convenience often lead to choices that aren't in line with your goals.

Do I really need to count calories?

Counting calories can feel overwhelming, especially if you have a busy lifestyle. Simple strategies, like focusing on protein and planning meals, can help without the need for tracking every number.

How do I find time to eat healthy?

It’s not about finding extra time; it’s about making small adjustments. Planning ahead, keeping healthy snacks on hand, and expecting those busy days will make a significant difference in your choices.
