Simple Strategy

  • 1. Focus on portion control: Use smaller plates or bowls to naturally reduce the amount you eat without feeling deprived.
  • 2. Prioritize protein: Include a source of protein in every meal or snack; this keeps you feeling full and reduces cravings.
  • 3. Stay hydrated: Drink a glass of water before meals; often, we mistake thirst for hunger, leading to unnecessary snacking.

What This Looks Like

Imagine it's a typical Wednesday. You wake up late due to an exhausting week, skip breakfast, and rush through your day. At lunch, your coworkers invite you out, and you end up choosing a burger and fries because it’s convenient. After work, you’re too tired to cook and order takeout instead, grabbing something that feels quick. Before you know it, you’ve eaten more than you intended, and your energy is low. Now you're feeling guilty but stuck in a cycle that makes real change feel impossible. Sound familiar?

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Skipping meals: It may seem like a quick fix, but missing meals can lead to overeating later.
  • 2. Relying on convenience foods: They might save time but often pack hidden sugars and unhealthy fats.
  • 3. Not planning ahead: Without a rough idea of what you’ll eat during the week, it’s easy to fall into bad habits.

These strategies work best when personalized to your lifestyle and goals.
