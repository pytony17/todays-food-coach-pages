Simple Strategy

  • 1. Start your day with a protein-rich breakfast like eggs or Greek yogurt to keep you satisfied longer.
  • 2. Prepare simple, make-ahead lunches like salads or wraps, so you’re not tempted to grab fast food during the day.
  • 3. For dinner, aim for one plate meals that combine lean protein (like chicken or fish), a vegetable, and a whole grain (like brown rice or quinoa) without overcomplicating it.

What This Looks Like

Imagine this: You wake up late and rush out the door, skipping breakfast. At noon, you have a quick meeting and opt for a burger and fries with your colleagues. After work, you’re exhausted and the thought of cooking is the last thing on your mind, so you order takeout again. It’s convenient, but you're not feeling any healthier. Later, you realize you missed another day of eating well—it's frustrating, but it's the reality of a busy life where every meal feels like a chore.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Skipping meals thinking you're saving calories, but it leads to unhealthy snacking later.
  • 2. Relying on fast food or convenience meals too often because they fit your busy schedule.
  • 3. Ignoring hunger cues and eating only when you have time, resulting in overeating later.
