Simple Strategy

  • 1. Fill half your plate with vegetables at each meal. This keeps things simple and helps you feel full without counting calories.
  • 2. Swap sugary drinks for water or herbal tea. It’s an easy change that can significantly reduce calorie intake.
  • 3. Keep healthy snacks on hand. Nuts, yogurt, or fruit make it easy to avoid unhealthy quick bites.

What This Looks Like

Imagine your typical day: you wake up late and skip breakfast because you're rushing to get the kids to school. At lunch, you grab a burger and fries because you don’t have time to prepare something healthy. After work, you’re exhausted and concede to pizza for dinner. Even though you intended to eat better, life got in the way. These choices add up, but it doesn’t have to be that way. Small, intentional tweaks can make all the difference over time.

Common Mistakes

  • 2. Having a 'clean plate' mentality that leads to overeating, even when you're full.
  • 3. Skipping meals, thinking it saves calories, but ending up overdoing it later.
