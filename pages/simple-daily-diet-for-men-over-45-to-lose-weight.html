Simple Strategy

  • 1. Prioritize protein: Start your day with a protein-rich breakfast like eggs or a Greek yogurt smoothie. It keeps you full and helps curb cravings.
  • 2. Make easy swaps: Opt for whole grain bread instead of white, or choose grilled chicken over fried when eating out. Small changes can make a big difference.
  • 3. Stay hydrated: Keep a water bottle handy and aim for at least 8 glasses a day. Often, thirst can be mistaken for hunger.

What This Looks Like

Imagine waking up late and skipping breakfast in the rush to get to work. By lunchtime, you're starving and grab a sandwich from the deli, but it's piled high with mayo and cheese. After another long day, you find yourself at a restaurant with friends. You know you should choose a salad, but the burger is calling your name. You leave feeling stuffed and frustrated about your choices, thinking you’ll do better tomorrow. Just like that, your good intentions slip away, turning into another day of unhealthy decisions.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Overcomplicating meals: Trying to prepare gourmet dishes can lead to burnout. Simple, satisfying meals are more doable.
  • 2. Skipping meals: When you're busy, it's tempting to skip meals—this can lead to overeating later. Regular meals help maintain energy and curb cravings.
  • 3. Ignoring hunger cues: Eating out of boredom or stress instead of hunger can quickly sabotage your progress. Learn to listen to your body.

Frequently Asked Questions
