Simple Strategy

  • 1. Keep it simple: Stock your fridge with no-cook ingredients like pre-washed salad greens, cooked rotisserie chicken, and canned beans. These can be thrown together for a quick meal.
  • 2. Invest in quality frozen meals: Look for nutritious, balanced frozen options that are low in calories but high in flavor. They’re quick to prepare and can support your weight loss journey.
  • 3. Plan for leftovers: Make extra servings when you do cook, so you have a ready-to-eat meal for the evening when you’re too tired to prepare anything.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Thinking cooking takes too long: Sometimes it feels like a chore, but it can be as quick as 10 minutes if you have the right ingredients on hand.
  • 2. Relying too much on takeout: While it’s convenient, those choices often pack on unnecessary calories and won’t help with your weight loss goals.
  • 3. Ignoring meal prep: Not taking a weekend to prep easy meals leads to last-minute decisions that can derail your diet.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don’t have time to cook healthy meals?

You don’t need to cook elaborate meals. Focus on quick, no-cook options or easy recipes that take under 20 minutes to prepare.

Can frozen meals be healthy?

Absolutely! Look for frozen meals that are balanced, high in protein, and packed with veggies to support your weight loss goals.

How can I keep track of my meals without counting calories?
