Simple Strategy

  • 1. Stock your fridge with easy-to-prepare foods like pre-cut veggies, rotisserie chicken, or canned beans for quick meals.
  • 2. Keep healthy snacks on hand, like nuts or yogurt, to curb your hunger before dinner and reduce impulse takeout.
  • 3. Dedicate one day a week for simple meal prep; make enough for a few days to save time and stress after work.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don’t have time to meal prep?

Even a small amount of prep can go a long way. Aim for just one hour a week to chop veggies or cook a base like quinoa or brown rice, which can be used in multiple meals.

Are pre-packaged healthy meals a good option?

Absolutely! Look for options with clean ingredients—these can save you time and still fit your health goals.

What should I keep on hand for quick dinners?

Think easy proteins like rotisserie chicken or canned tuna, as well as frozen veggies and whole grains. These can come together into a meal in less than 20 minutes.
