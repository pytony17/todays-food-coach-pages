Simple Strategy

  • 1. Choose grilled over fried: When scanning the menu, go for grilled meats or veggies. They’re lower in calories and higher in nutrients.
  • 2. Customize your order: Don’t hesitate to ask for modifications, like dressing on the side or swapping fries for a salad. It’s your meal!
  • 3. Share or control portions: Consider sharing an entrée or asking for a lunch-size portion. You’ll enjoy the flavors without overdoing it.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Skipping meals before eating out: Arriving too hungry often leads you to make poor choices off the menu.
  • 2. Ignoring how drinks add up: Cocktails and sugary drinks can add hundreds of calories and sabotage your efforts.
  • 3. Relying on 'healthy' labels: Just because something is labeled 'healthy' doesn't mean it's low in calories. Always check menu items carefully.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I can't avoid fast food?

Fast food doesn't have to mean unhealthy. Look for grilled items, salads, and avoid large portions if you must eat out.

Are there weight loss-friendly options at all restaurants?

Most restaurants offer healthier alternatives. Just focus on grilled, baked, or steamed options and opt for side salads.

How do I make good choices when I feel tired?

Plan ahead when possible. Choose restaurants with healthy options you know, and don't hesitate to ask for modifications.
