Simple Strategy

  • 1. Choose grilled over fried: When opting for protein, select grilled chicken or fish instead of fried options. It’s often more flavorful and packs fewer calories.
  • 2. Opt for salads: Go for salads with a protein source, and ask for dressings on the side. You control how much you use!
  • 3. Skip the sides: Say no to bread or chips as starters. Focus on the main dish and keep those empty calories at bay.

What This Looks Like

Imagine this: You’ve had a long day at work, and it’s 7 PM with no dinner plan in sight. Feeling too drained to cook, you decide to hit your favorite burger joint. You glance at the menu and notice the tempting combo meals. Instead of drowning in options, you remember to ask for a grilled chicken sandwich instead of a burger, hold the fries, and get a side salad. It’s not perfect—you're still dining out, but you've taken a step in the right direction amidst your busy schedule.

Common Mistakes

  • 1. Ignoring portion sizes: Many dishes come in sizes meant for sharing. Going large can pack on extra calories without you even noticing.
  • 2. Drinking calories: Sugary drinks or even fancy coffees can rack up calories quickly. Opt for water or unsweetened tea to save calories.
  • 3. Getting swayed by specials: Restaurants often promote items that can be high in calories. Stick to your go-to healthy options instead.

These strategies work best when personalized to your lifestyle and goals.
